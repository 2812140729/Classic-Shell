(...I don't know if it is another Win7 "feature" or a side-effect of Classic Shell...)

Take the following steps (forget your mouse; use only the keyboard):
- Open Windows Explorer with WinButton+E keyboard shortcut.
- Navigate to any folder in the right pane.
- Check any item in the right pane; leave it highlighted.
- Press Tab about 4 times until you highlight the parent folder in the left pane.
The item selection in the right pane looks active, but it is not; the active selection is in the left pane...

So, if you marked a single file in the right pane e.g. for deletion (and you are a tired enough), it is possible to delete the entire directory from the left pane!

(...I was lucky enough to find the folder in the Recycle Bin...)

Any ideas?

[edit]
...And if you bring another window in the foreground and then revert to Windows Explorer, even the dotted frame from the left pane selection disappears!!
