I installed windows solitaire and classic shell broke the link to the icon on the metro desktop. My fault, but here's how I did it:

After installing classic shell, I saw the "Apps" program group right above the search box (neat!). I thought to myself, "I'll just drag this one app icon from the Apps program group to the desktop and that will be my desktop shortcut." Innocent enough... Wrong. The resulting desktop shortcut didn't work, and by dragging it out of the App group it's gone, and apparently corrupts the linkage between the actual app tile and the Metro desktop as well.

I can't reinstall the app. Windows store says I have it.
Couldn't uninstall the app. No uninstall button was available.
Clicking on the tile just opened the store not the app itself, so I unpinned it.

Any suggestions on how one might

Repair links to missing apps on the metro desktop?
Or allow it to be uninstalled and reinstalled from the store?
And how I can rebuild the link in the Apps program group?

Here is how you can fix it. Open this folder: %localappdata%\Microsoft\Windows\Application Shortcuts. Locate the folder of the Modern app there and move the shortcut on the desktop back inside that folder (cut-paste). Then it will show up in "Apps" in the Start Menu.

To get it back on the Start screen, you may have to restart Explorer.exe or logoff/login back.
