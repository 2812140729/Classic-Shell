I also have this kind of icon-flickering on the desktop.
But I can tell you when it happens and how to "workaround" it.

For ME it has to do with Classic Shell. Maybe in cooperation with other programs...

The flickering happens when "Disable Active Corners" is set to "All" and the mouse is moved to the top of the screen. It only happens when the mouse is at the top. When I move a few pixels away, it stops.
The flickering looks like a random "Morse Code" and not something "linear".
If I enable Active Corners a Hand-Cursor appears when moved to the top of the screen and no flickering occurs.

I am using Classic Shell 3.6.5 on Windows 8 64 Bit.

I hope that helps.

This is the only but an annoying problem.

Apart from this, Classic Shell is awesome! Keep up the good Work!
