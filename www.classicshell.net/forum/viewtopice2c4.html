DarkSpore Pie2 (Classic Style 1 & 2 ) (a.k.a Dog's breakfast)

More or less finished the skin. It ends up with a rough dirty look.
I made the skin from two bitmaps,
of the Pie folder, from Splitwirez.

The blue menu can become very wide, due to its many borders, especially in single mode. I count 7 big borders.
Had it thinner at one time in the process, just didn't have the same character, so left as big, and powerful looking.

Another aspect that stays big, is the height of all menus.
It's because of the way the stretch points, had to be, to keep most of the old fashion tv type look.
I had also reduced the height size, while processing,
then switched back to a compromise to keep enough of those aliasing lines as possible.
The problem with this is evident mostly on single menus, with "small icons".
If there are not enough entries in the menu, there will be an empty space at bottom of menu.
Even moving "Column Padding" in "Customize Start Menu" does not work.
One thing that does work is to drag a couple "custom"s and drop just above the separator.
For me, I just let it do it's thing, as menu fills up with entries, looks normal again.

Why not all docked in the corner?
The main menu, has tiny running lights, and edges that at times, glow a bit, with a dark background.
One can't see them all, when docked.






Feels like a marriage.
Do you column in black, take this column, dressed in blue,
to live happily ever after, along with your submenu little one?
Apparently not.
I see them as singles, in the one column section!
I like the single black menu best.

Make sure to turn off menu shadow in Classic Shell Settings.
Had to zip skin, as it got so big with all the variations.
I have no idea if the lines create interference on other screen resolutions. Mine is 1920 x1080

Edit: Fixed wrong bitmap reference

DarkSpore Pie3




A New Year, and a new piece of pie skin.

After many failed attempts, got the alpha transparency some what acceptable.
Lots of intentional overlaps, and the most 45 degree angles,
for any skin that's gone past me.
Those split selector buttons, can be quite a puzzle, to work out the odd shapes.
After more tries, than buying lottery tickets, I nailed it.

I put some preset color menus in skin. This was the fun part, making them so saturated with color, & tint shifts.
Pushed the saturation pink drive the most, just short of exploding.
They all have more dynamic range, than what glass color can provide.

As the skin has smokey blue tinge, and using Win10 limited color table,
the added glass option is best for just blue varitions. Other glass window colors don't blend nicely.
It can be overidden though, with Ivo's excellent "Overide glass color" in "Menu Look" tab.
I kept the glass intensity low, to keep glass from washing out the details.

Turn off menu shadow, to get rid of right vertical line. I put a shadow/glow on edge to make up for loss.

For the new apps/programs button, I borrowed some rainbowness from Pipalou50. Thanx.




OK, Splitwirez.
Your turn

EDIT: I added a dark pure grey option, with that bitmap,
used in combination with the mild glass option.
