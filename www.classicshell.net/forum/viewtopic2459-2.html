Experiment # 2 Handhelds

Major tweaking of Xp skin.
Time to break out of flatland for awhile.
I see everyone thumbing through their phones and pads.
A spark went off in my brain, when I was holding a bar.
I wondered if I could make it into the menu columns,
and incorporate Windows colors to make them look like something?

Behold. Handhelds, for desktop users.

The highlighted selection color looks like it wraps around the sides.
Gives it some depth.
At first the menu looked really bad.
All the noise in bitmap picture, would stretch and make vertical lines.
I really had to denoise it a lot.
Had to learn how not to stretch the corners, by adjusting the slices.
It's not a perfect illusion.
I'm sure graphic artists can improve on it with all their 3D plugins.

Tried with CSM 4.0.2 on Win7/8
Not compatible with most older versions of CSM. Will only partially work.
After downloading file, paste in skins folder. Exit CSM, restart CSM,
and go to "Start Menu Style" tab. Pick Classic or Classic two columns.
Pick "Handhelds" in Skin tab.

ps. Feel free to change anything in this skin, especially my errors, as it's just an experiment.
Upload a better one. Nothing is permanent, everything changes:)

I don't even know what the percentage relates to.
Is it log, exponential, or conversion of alpha ?/255 bits?
Which of the six types and is it for Win7 or 8?

This is what I did, I made the skin as a quick reference to what plain transparencies looks like.

Made a variation 1
I made the selectors and separators at 128/255 A
The main black part of menu at 216/255 A. I guess is around 15 %
The tops and bottoms at 235/255 A
A few edges at 250/255A.

Variation 2 has main menu at 128/255 A.

Variation 3 is 1/255 A.

I put a transparency selection group in skin options,
so you, me or anybody can see what each does.

Looks like alpha and glass, make the tops and bottom transparent, but not the main black menu.
Full alpha, and full glass make everything transparent.
Solid-transparent mixes to the background menu color. In this case white = grey.
Region seems to let the transparent edge dots show desktop, and block everything else, making a little darker.

Here is where it gets weird.
Win7
Fullalpha same
Fullglass same brightness as fullalpha, but with blur.

Win8
Fullalpha same
Fullglass darker, no blur.

I didn't give you exactly what you wanted.
Gave you the building blocks though.

Every body has different tastes for see through menus. Also, might need to adjust the font smoothing, in CSM.
I see you like extreme. Just remember, white on dark desktop background looks great,
until a white window appears = whiteout
